
# GoodCabs Operations Analysis

## Project Overview

GoodCabs is a cab service company operating across 10 tier-2 cities in India. Founded in 2022, the company aims to become the leading mobility provider in tier-2 markets while maintaining a balance between profitability and community support.

This project analyzes trip demand, passenger behavior, revenue performance, customer satisfaction, and target achievement metrics to uncover business opportunities and support strategic decision-making.

---

##  Business Objective

The objective of this analysis was to:

* Evaluate city-level operational performance.
* Analyze passenger acquisition and retention trends.
* Understand revenue and pricing patterns.
* Identify demand seasonality and travel behavior.
* Assess monthly target achievement.
* Provide actionable recommendations for business growth.

---

##  Problem Statement

GoodCabs set ambitious performance goals for 2024 and required a comprehensive analysis of:

* Trip Volume
* Repeat Passenger Rate
* Passenger Satisfaction
* Revenue Performance
* Trip Distribution Patterns
* New vs Repeat Passenger Trends
* Monthly Target Achievement

The insights were intended to help the Chief of Operations make informed strategic decisions and improve overall business performance.

## Key Insights

* Jaipur emerged as the strongest market, contributing 34.39% of total revenue while recording the highest trip volume.
* Lucknow and Surat generated strong demand and exhibited the highest repeat passenger rates, indicating high customer dependency on the service.
* Revenue was heavily concentrated in Jaipur, creating potential business risk from over-reliance on a single market.
* Mysore maintained some of the highest customer ratings despite having the lowest trip volume, demonstrating strong service quality.
* Repeat passengers consistently rated the service lower than new passengers, suggesting service inconsistencies and rising customer expectations.
* Demand patterns revealed two distinct segments: tourism-driven cities (Jaipur, Kochi, Mysore) and commuter-driven cities (Surat, Lucknow, Vadodara).
* Most cities experienced peak demand between February and May, while June emerged as the weakest demand month.
* Surat, Lucknow, and Vadodara showed strong customer retention despite relatively low satisfaction scores.
* Mysore consistently exceeded trip and rating targets, while Surat, Lucknow, and Vadodara struggled to meet performance goals.
* High-frequency riders were concentrated in Surat, Lucknow, and Vadodara, reinforcing their role as commuter-centric markets.

---

##  Strategic Recommendations

### Improve Service Quality

* Strengthen driver training programs.
* Conduct regular service quality audits.
* Analyze customer complaints and feedback trends.
* Prioritize improvements in Surat, Lucknow, and Vadodara.

### Expand Tourism Partnerships

* Build partnerships with hotels, airports, resorts, and tourism operators.
* Focus expansion efforts in Jaipur, Kochi, and Mysore.

### Develop Corporate Mobility Programs

* Target commuter-heavy markets such as Surat, Lucknow, and Vadodara.
* Partner with business districts, IT parks, manufacturing hubs, and universities.

### Increase Customer Acquisition

* Expand referral programs and promotional campaigns.
* Improve digital marketing efforts in Jaipur and other growth markets.

### Reduce Revenue Concentration Risk

* Accelerate growth initiatives in Kochi, Surat, and Lucknow.
* Diversify revenue sources to reduce dependence on Jaipur.

### Adopt City-Specific Growth Strategies

* Develop tourism-focused strategies for Jaipur, Kochi, and Mysore.
* Implement commuter-focused retention and mobility programs in Surat, Lucknow, and Vadodara.
